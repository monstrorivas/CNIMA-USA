Nathalie Boucheix

Nathalie Boucheix is an accordionist and the director of the CNIMA, which she co-founded in 1995 with her former teacher, Jacques Mornet.

Winner of some of the world’s most prestigious accordion competitions — the Trophée Mondial (1994) and Klingenthal (1994) — she also received the distinguished Médard Ferrero Prize (1993) and the SACEM Francis Baxter Prize (1993). With a Diploma of Musical Studies (DEM) in accordion, she quickly established herself as a brilliant and exacting performer.

Nathalie has appeared in many of France’s grand gala events alongside legendary accordionists such as André Verchuren, Maurice Larcange, and Yvette Horner. She also performs internationally, offering recitals that blend concert-style variety with refined, contemporary colors.

At CNIMA, she serves as administrative director, pedagogical director, and teacher of both accordion and musicianship. A recognized expert in instrumental technique and musical interpretation, she trains musicians from all backgrounds in both classical repertoire and concert-style variety. Together with Jacques Mornet, she regularly leads workshops and masterclasses in France and abroad.

In 2025, at the Coupe Mondiale, Nathalie Boucheix received the CIA Merit Award from the Confédération Internationale des Accordéonistes (CIA), honoring her exceptional contribution to the accordion’s international reach. This prestigious distinction has previously been awarded to renowned figures such as Russian master Friedrich Lips and world-famous virtuoso Richard Galliano.
